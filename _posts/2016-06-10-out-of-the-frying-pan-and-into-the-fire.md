To say the paradigm has shifted is an understatement. The real part of the course started on Monday and the insensity is a bit staggering. All the same, the challenge has been exhilerating. I believe the next few months are going to test me in ways that I haven't even begun to comprehend. 

### Objects

We're back into Objective-C. In the first lab we were given a peak at what was possible with the UI aspects of app development, but since then we're back to the commandline output, which is a bit of a come down but is probably for the best. The lectures have been mostly review, but we have started to move into Object Orient Programming. My impressions of Flatiron is like this: it's like buying an expensive model kit. The parts are nicely constructed, and of high quality. The instructions are clear and concise but at the end of the day you still need to put everything together. You still get out of it what you put in. 


### More Ice Breakers Than Russia...

Everyone I've met so far have been pretty awesome. The rest of the iOS immersive students finally joined the Mobile Dev Corps students and the class is finally complete. There seems to be a nice energy and mix of people from different backgrounds to create a really dynamic and positive group. The school has had us doing a ton of 'ice breakers' as a combined iOS and Webdev group.
